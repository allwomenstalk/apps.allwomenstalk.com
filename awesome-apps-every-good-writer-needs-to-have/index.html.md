# 7 Awesome Apps Every Good Writer Needs to Have ...

There are so many fantastic apps for writers. If you're always looking for an easier way to write poetry or simply collect your thoughts, these apps will definitely come in handy. I actually have a few of these that do wonders for my college writing courses! Here are some of the best apps for writers:

## 1. Poetreat

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_
**Price:** Free

Have you ever found yourself, despite your lack of knowledge about it, wanting to write poetry? Well, this app can help you out. It's a poetry editor that will suggest rhymes to you as you write! With the app, you can discover your inner poet and then share your poems with your friends or your special someone by connecting to Twitter or Facebook! This is just one of many awesome apps for writers.

## 2. Merriam-webster Dictionary

![Merriam-webster Dictionary](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/zc/dc/etcz6w54_320x568.jpg)

**On iTunes at:** [itunes.apple.com](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/merriam-webster-dictionary/id399452287?amp%3Bat=11l4BD)
**Price:** Free

If you're a writer, I'm sure you've needed a dictionary every now and then. With this app, it makes things a lot more convenient. The app even offers voice search to let you look up a word without having to spell it! It's a great tool for vocabulary building because it doubles as a thesaurus and has cool features like "Word of the Day" and more! By looking at your recent history, you can even keep track of all the words you've looked up.

## 3. Evernote

![Evernote](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/eu/q9/t1ckum51_319x568.jpg)

**On iTunes at:** [itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/DLP0YU)
**Price:** Free

This app is essential for anyone who needs a place to store and organize all their thoughts, media, and information. What's awesome is that the contents of EverNote can be synced across all devices, providing access anytime/anywhere. The app has even dubbed as the "modern workplace that helps you be your most productive self." How can you go wrong with that?

## 4. Dragon Diction

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_
**Price:** Free

You know those times where you have to text your friend a REALLY long story? It can be so annoying having to type everything. Dragon Diction solves all your problems! All you have to do is a tap a button, and everything you say turns into text. Awesome, right? On the app, you can also dictate status updates directly to your social networking app likes Facebook and Twitter, or send notes and reminders to yourself!

## 5. Songza

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_
**Price:** Free

Everyone needs a good playlist! That's how I manage to get all my best work done. What's so cool about Songza is that all you have to do is let it know what you're up to. They'll play you music based on mood, decade, or the genre of music. I honestly don't know how I lived without this app - it always finds me the right music at the right time. Depending on whether you get your work done by listening to classical music, or some rock n' roll, Songza will help.

## 6. Spice Mobile

![Spice Mobile](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ff/wb/nhsj7vuh_332x568.jpg)

**On iTunes at:** [itunes.apple.com](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/spice-mobile/id519101876?amp%3Bat=11l4BD)
**Price:** Free

Having writer's block? There's an easy fix and it's to add some spice in your life! The app offers in-app purchases like a monthly subscription, which will provide you with on-the-go access to their phrase thesaurus, which has over 22,000 creative phrases. It's a must-have for every writer!

## 7. A Novel Idea

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_
**Price:** Free

If you're serious about your writing, this app would be great for you. It's the premiere tool for plotting out your story and recording all your bursts of inspiration!

Well, there yo have it. These are just seven out of many awesome apps for writers. If you're a writer, which app helps YOU out the most?
