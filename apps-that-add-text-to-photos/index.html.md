# 7 Apps That Add Text to Photos ...

Apps that add text to photos can add another element to your favourite shots. They can help your photos tell a story or you can use them to watermark your images. Luckily, there are plenty of apps on the market that allow you to easily add text to all your phone photos. Have you ever wondered about those apps that add text to photos on your smart phone? Then check out the next couple of nifty apps.

## 1. Over

![Over](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/apps/2013/05/1_over_270x480.jpg)

[itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/25rRhP)
First up on the list of apps that add text to photos is Over. The app is simple to use and lets you add beautiful type to your image. Simply snap or upload an image from your phone and type in your text. The app comes with 28 different fonts and purchasing an in-app upgrade will also give you access to more fonts. The Over app costs $1.99 from the iTunes app store.

## 2. PicLab

![PicLab](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/apps/2013/05/2_piclab_270x480.jpg)

[itunes.apple.com](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/piclab-add-beautiful-text/id597851882)
PicLab is another app that lets you add text to your photos. As well as text, you can also add different masks. These masks can range from shapes and borders to light leaks. The app is free to download from the iTunes app store, but does publish photos with a noticeable watermark. Purchasing the in-app upgrade for $0.99 will remove the watermark as well as giving you access to more features.

## 3. Instanote

![Instanote](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/apps/2013/05/3_instanote_320x480.jpg)

[itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/0N4Hm2)
Unlike the previous two apps, Instanote is available for free download on the iTunes app store and Google Play. The app lets you choose from 9 different note styles and 11 different fonts. You can also play around with the size, opacity, and colour of the text. In-app upgrades can also be purchased to give you access to a greater range of fonts and background styles.

## 4. Labelbox

_ itunes.apple.com_
The Labelbox app is a bit different in that it allows you to add text to your photos in the form of different labels. Add ribbon, tape, or candy wrapper style labels to your images. Simply swipe the image where you want the label to go and type in your text. This app is free to download for iPhone and Android phones.

## 5. Overgram

_ itunes.apple.com_
Overgram is the free version of the Over app that is previously mentioned on the list. As a result, it’s pretty similar in design. The Overgram app comes with 10 different fonts and you can can easily resize, align, and position your text where you want it. The only downside is that photos are published with a noticeable watermark.

## 6. LINE Camera

![LINE Camera](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/apps/2013/05/6_line-camera_270x480.jpg)

[itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/fKwTw7)
If you prefer your apps a bit more quirky and boisterous then try out LINE Camera. This app lets you decorate your photos with filters and text. The app has everything from filters, borders, stamps, and text. It’s currently free to download on the iTunes app store and Google Play, with in-app purchases costing extra.

## 7. Phonto

![Phonto](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/apps/2013/05/7_phonto_270x480.jpg)

[itunes.apple.com](https://apps.apple.com/au/app/phonto-text-on-image/id438429273)
Phonto is a free app that lets you add text to your images. The app comes with a range of different fonts, but if you’re using iOS devices you can also copy font files between your computer and your phone. The app is available for iPhone and Android phones and is free to download. However, the iPhone version has noticeably more features than the Android one.

These are just a few handy apps to help you add text to your photos. Do you know of any other apps that let you add text to your photos? What are your favourite smart phone apps?
