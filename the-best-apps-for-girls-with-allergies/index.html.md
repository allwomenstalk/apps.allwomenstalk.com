# The 7 Best Allergy Apps to Track Symptoms and Avoid Triggers

Allergies can be incredibly frustrating. Hay fever makes spring and summer a challenge with itchy, streaming eyes and irritated noses. Allergies to pets are inconvenient at best, and food allergies can make eating out feel like navigating a minefield. However, help is at hand. While medication is crucial, finding the **best allergy app** can transform how you manage your daily life. From the **best allergy tracker app** to pollen alerts, these digital tools are designed to keep you informed and safe.

Whether you're dealing with seasonal sniffles or strict dietary requirements, the **best allergy apps** offer personalized insights. Below is a helpful overview of what these apps can offer before we dive into the specifics:

| Feature Type | Why It Helps | Ideal For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Allergy Trackers** | Logs symptoms to identify patterns over time. | Chronic allergy sufferers |
| **Pollen Alerts** | Provides daily forecasts to plan outdoor activities. | Seasonal hay fever |
| **Food Scanners** | Checks ingredients quickly to prevent reactions. | Food allergies & sensitivities |

## 1. Allergy Alert

![Allergy Alert App Screenshot](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/jk/r6/ovjoovav573c95e0b3bf4891929374_500x572.jpg)

One of the best apps for an environmental allergy is definitely the Allergy Alert app. It is a free-to-download application that displays a large number of highly accurate forecasts for things like pollen count and UV radiation. If you are someone who is susceptible to hay fever or severe sunburn, consulting this app for a full 5-day forecast can be extremely helpful when planning days out with friends or family. Staying ahead of the weather is easier when you have the [best pollen apps](https://weather.com/health/allergy) at your fingertips.

## 2. WebMD Allergy

![WebMD Allergy App Interface](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/pg/w5/jor01ud1573c95e11dd53426258496_500x572.jpg)

This app is a lifesaver, sometimes quite literally. Many of us already look up our symptoms online, so this **WebMD allergy app** version is specifically dedicated to helping you get to the bottom of your allergy problems. The app allows you to keep a detailed journal-type log of your symptoms, noting which parts of the day they are at their worst and in which locations. It can provide a fantastic resource in learning which places and things to avoid. You can find more details on the [official WebMD allergies page](https://www.webmd.com/allergies/default.htm).

## 3. SafeEats

![SafeEats App Display](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ft/de/j9m2cyp3573c95e158daf418887077_500x572.jpg)

SafeEats is an amazing app that can truly be the difference between feeling healthy and dealing with an unexpected reaction. The app allows you to scan the barcode of any food item in a grocery store that you are interested in buying. Within seconds, it will be able to tell you whether the product contains any of the ingredients that you have inputted into your allergy profile when you first downloaded it. It is an essential tool for anyone managing strict dietary restrictions.

## 4. MedHelper

![MedHelper Dashboard](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/eb/j2/yexmdqbm573c95e12edd8824210566_500x572.jpg)

Living with a large number of allergies can be very time-consuming when it comes to the different medications you have to take. The MedHelper app helps you to stay on top of this by giving you a dedicated space to manage and schedule all of your different prescriptions, ensuring you never run out or take too much of something by mistake. You can create an online inventory, which will let you know exactly when it is time to restock your supplies. Keeping track of medications is just as important as finding the [best app for allergies](#1) like pollen.

## 5. ZYRTEC AllergyCast

![ZYRTEC AllergyCast App](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ru/vv/lpkty01m573c95e112bc0140717231_500x560.jpg)

This is another popular application that is accurate in predicting forecasts for the places you live in and near. With a variety of options and tools to play with, you can tweak and customize the forecasts to provide you with the information that is most important regarding your personal allergies. You can also set favorite locations for a quick look before heading out. It is consistently rated as one of the best allergy tracker apps available. Check out the [official Zyrtec app page](https://www.zyrtec.com/allergycast-app) for more features.

## 6. Substitutions

![Substitutions App View](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/a4/ll/uj5eboqy573c95e14ca83563787709_500x572.jpg)

This handy app will help you out when you are cooking a recipe and all of a sudden see an ingredient listed that you know you are allergic to. Simply whip out your smartphone, type the offending ingredient into the app, and within seconds you will be presented with a list of alternatives. These options ensure you avoid allergens while finding substitutes that will complement the dish perfectly. It makes cooking at home significantly less stressful.

## 7. AllergyFT

![AllergyFT App Interface](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/x3/en/avgmwdtp573c95e1a9c4b516205381_500x568.jpg)

Food allergies can be a really tricky thing to handle when you are traveling in another country. This app lets you create an allergy profile for yourself and any friends and family you are vacationing with, which can then be translated into the language of the country you are visiting. It takes the guesswork out of ordering food abroad and ensures you can communicate your needs clearly to restaurant staff.
