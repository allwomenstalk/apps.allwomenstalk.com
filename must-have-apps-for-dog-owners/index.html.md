# 7 Must Have Apps for Dog Owners ...

Having a pet is a big responsibility, but you can get some help and advice from apps for dog owners that mean you can give your pooch the best care and the best life. Apps for dog owners provide advice on every aspect of looking after your beloved four-legged friend, from diet to exercise to training and even doggie vacations. Check out these canine-loving apps.

## 1. Puppy Coach

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_

If training your puppy seems like a daunting task to you, there are apps for dog owners to make your life easier. Puppy Coach breaks down the training sets into nine topics that are quite easy to follow. Each topic has its own instructional videos that are short and manageable, and are led by a trainer. As you complete each topic, you can check it off and keep measuring your performance. You can even print a diploma at the end of the course for your puppy that’ll certainly deserve some special treatment if it has come so far!

## 2. MapMyDogwalk

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_

If you needed one app for you and your puppy, this might be it. Actually, it encourages both of you to get moving and adopt a healthy lifestyle. It not only logs your route but also provides extra information like time, speed and distance, all in real time. Health freaks can even find out how many calories they’ve burnt and can even manually enter workout data from indoor activities. If you and your canine companion want to track your health progress together, then this is one of the best apps for dog owners.

## 3. DoggyDatez

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_

If ever there was a Facebook style social presence for dogs, then this is it. You can easily check out if there are any dogs that do their walks in the nearby areas. Setting up dates with other puppies isn’t a bad idea, especially if it helps you meet new people as well.

## 4. Dog Park Finder plus

![Dog Park Finder plus](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/bn/ln/hs5mwzn0_320x568.jpg)

**On iTunes at:** [itunes.apple.com](https://goo.gl/3upSKO)

Dog Park Finder Plus is a database of parks, beaches and other places of interest for your dog. You can view and choose from more than 6000 dog friendly places. You’ll even find other useful information, such as if it’s free to enter or not, if it has the desired safety measures installed and also when it opens and closes. This makes it amongst the perfect apps for dog owners.

## 5. BringFido

![BringFido](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vg/am/zq9lw5os_320x568.jpg)

**On iTunes at:** [itunes.apple.com](https://apps.apple.com/de/app/bringfido-pet-friendly-hotels/id682820712?amp%3Bat=11l4BD)

This one is your dog’s very own travel agent. You can search for pet-friendly hotels and accommodations and even book your room through the app. You have the facility to filter your search by popularity, distance, price and rating. You can even read the pet policy for each hotel, which means you won’t have last minute surprises when you check-in!

## 6. Tagg

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_

Nobody wants to lose their dog. I still remember people putting up ads of their lost dogs hoping someone would find them and bring them home. That is a thing of the past now. With Tagg, you can use GPS tracking to locate the position of your beloved pooch. You can also monitor your dog’s daily activity, thus ensuring that he is active all day and when not, you at least know something is wrong with him.

## 7. Pet First Aid

_ **On iTunes at:** itunes.apple.com_

This one is your dog’s first aid kit. Rich with life saving information and instructional videos, the app ensures you have all the necessary things you need to ensure your dog’s health in time of need. I hope you never have to run into an emergency with your dog but when you do, you’ll thank the creator of this app for the marvelous gift.

You have apps to help organize your life, apps to help you be a better person and probably apps associated with your health, so it makes sense to make use of the apps for dog owners too. Agreed?
