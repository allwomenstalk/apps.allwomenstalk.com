# 5 Best Food and Drink Apps for 2019 ...

If there is one thing that Millennials love more than themselves, it is food and drink! You only need to take a look at all the various culinary hashtags on Instagram to see that us young people often pay more attention to our meals and snacks than many other areas of life, and that is because nothing makes you feel great like a good dinner or a delicious new beverage discovery! To help you have more revelatory moments of your own like these, these are some of the **best food and drink apps** for 2019.A must-have for foodies.

## 1. Where Chefs Eat

_ If you want to know where all of the best places to dine are in your area, this app is the thing that can help you out. It is a recommendation-based app that isn’t made by just anyone, it is based on the opinions of actual chefs, and I think they know what they are talking about! Don’t waste time on Trip Advisor when you could be using this instead.

itunes.apple.com_
[play.google.com](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.phaidon.wherechefseat)

## 2. Eaten

_ This is perfect for you if you are the kind of person who likes to look at the menu before going to the restaurant! Rather than rating an entire place, the app rates individual dishes on the menu, so you can easily find out where in town has the best steak or the best desserts!

itunes.apple.com_
[play.google.com](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.eatentechnologies.Eaten)

## 3. Local Wine Events

![Red, Logo, Font, Line, Symbol,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/tb/qz/w2xqwzy05c75a4a172ba6664561509_600x315.jpg)

This is an app that is basically a ‘what’s on’ page for everything related to wine events and occasions in your local area. From sales at stores to wine tasting sessions to reviews of the latest bars opening up across town, this app pretty much has you completely clued in for everything up to a five-mile radius!

[itunes.apple.com](https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/wine-events/id352070012?mt=8)
[play.google.com](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.localwineevents.wine)

## 4. Ginventory

![Yellow, Font, Orange, Logo, Line,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vi/ih/dn7ndrm65c75a49fedb43479072580_600x315.jpg)

You don’t need me to tell you that gin is the in thing right now! It feels like a new gin is coming out every other week these days, and this app can tell you a little bit about each and every one of them. It is full of reviews and recommendations and pairing advice.
[itunes.apple.com](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/ginventory-gin-tonic-guide/id998106458)
[play.google.com](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=be.reed.ginventory)

## 5. Happy Cow

![Violet, Cartoon, Purple, Snout, Clip art,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wv/ro/c91vuu945c75a4a071a74717925686_600x315.jpg)

This is a trusted, well reputed online guide to everything vegan and vegetarian. Some places are hell if you aren’t a carnivore, but this app helps to make everything more easier for you by listing establishments from eateries to B&Bs that cater to your special dietary choices. No more going out with friends and having to settle for that one gross vegan dish on the menu!

[itunes.apple.com](https://itunes.apple.com/us/app/happycow-find-vegan-food/id435871950?mt=8)
[play.google.com](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.hcceg.veg.compassionfree)
