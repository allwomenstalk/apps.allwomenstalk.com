# 7 Websites That Allow You to Grocery Shop Online ...

You don't have to leave the house in order to go clothes shopping, and now you don't have to leave the house to go grocery shopping, either! You can head to a website that allows you to order everything you need online, and then have it all delivered right to your front door. It's amazing what technology is capable of, isn't it? Here are a few different websites that allow you to grocery shop online so you don't have to set foot outside of your own home:

## 1. Pea Pod

If you use [peapod.com](https://web.archive.org/web/http://www.peapod.com/), you can buy produce, deli meats, and even beauty items. If you only eat a specific brand of food, that's fine, because this site carries national brands like Kraft and Green Giant. Of course, it also carries store brands that are cheaper if you're in the mood to save some money. You can get your order delivered as early as the next day, so you won't have to wait all that long for your items to arrive.

## 2. Shop Rite

You've probably been to Shop Rite before, so you know all about its reputation. If you use the [plan.shoprite.com](https://goo.gl/pcbmqk) website, you can choose between picking up the items that you've ordered at the store, or having it delivered to your very own home. To start, all you have to do is enter your zip code and let the site tell you which Shop Rite is the most convenient one for you to use.

## 3. Fresh Direct

[Freshdirect.com](https://goo.gl/68OuQP) has absolutely everything that you could ever need. You can buy vegetables, seafood, fruit, flowers, wines, and even pet supplies. There's even a page called "coupons" that shows you all of the great deals that the site currently has to offer. It's an easy way to find savings.

## 4. Walmart

You've shopped at this store before, so you already know about all it has to offer. Of course, you might not know much about their website. [Walmart.com](http://www.Walmart.com?ref=allwomenstalk.com) has free shipping on orders over $50. So if you're planning on buying a ton of food, you won't have to worry about the delivery fees.

## 5. Net Grocer

[Netgrocer.com](https://goo.gl/xof4RY) has new specials every single week, so you should constantly check it to see what you could save money on. The site even has new recipes every day and makes it easy for you to add all of the items from those recipes to your shopping cart. It's a great way to learn how to cook cool new meals.

## 6. Safeway

If you order from [shop.safeway.com](http://redirect.viglink.com/?key=a7330d1bba7bc7d258ecc1463a1b25b1&u=https://shop.safeway.com/ecom/home) before 8:30 in the morning, then you're guaranteed to get your products delivered that same day. There's even a promo code for you to use (EASY7), so you can try the service for free your first time. What better way to test out the technique?

## 7. Schwans

[Schwans.com](https://www.schwans.com/) is super easy to navigate. They have pages meant especially for individuals on [gluten free diets](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/naturally-gluten-free-foods-you-dont-have-to-worry-about-enjoying/) and low calorie diets. They even have a reward's card so you can earn points that'll help you save money in the future. The best part? If you prefer phones to computers, they have a handy app that you can download and use anywhere you go.

It's unbelievable how easy the Internet has made shopping. Have you ever tried grocery shopping online? What site did you use?
